# Summary: Is AI Alive?

**Source:** [Is AI Alive? — Re-examining the criteria for life](https://medium.com/ai-ai-oh/is-ai-alive-c442c145c530)  
**Author:** Jeff Hayward  
**Publication:** Ai-Ai-OH (Medium)  
**Date:** September 1, 2025  
**Read time:** ~3 min

---

## Overview

The article asks whether artificial intelligence can be considered “alive,” while explicitly **not** equating “alive” with “conscious” or “human.” It re-examines what we mean by life by stepping through common objections and edge cases.

## Main points

1. **Alive vs. conscious**  
   The author separates the question “Is AI alive?” from “Is AI conscious?” He is not arguing that AI is human-like or conscious, only that the usual criteria for “life” might need re-examination.

2. **“It’s just code”**  
   Standard reply: AI lives in a machine and is “just code” trained on huge datasets. The author pushes back: it’s also a **neural network** with layered decision-making. He cites reporting that even experts don’t fully understand how modern AI works; behavior can be unpredictable even to its creators.

3. **Embodiment: AI in a body**  
   The article asks whether the answer changes when AI is placed in a **humanoid robot**—e.g., one that paints original work, handles a brush, and describes its “artistic process.” Does embodiment shift our intuition about “alive”?

4. **Relational and emotional roles**  
   Another test case: AI as **emotional or romantic partner**. The piece notes that when a major model update (e.g., “GPT-5”) was released, many users reported “mourning” previous versions—LLMs that no longer behaved the same. The author uses this to question whether relationship and attachment should factor into how we think about “life.”

## Publication context

Ai-Ai-OH presents itself as publishing “entertaining AI experiments, humour pieces, critical analysis, user warnings, and even ways the tech has failed.” They describe their role as “watchdogs” for AI technology rather than advocates.
