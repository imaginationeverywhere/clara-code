# Augusta — Augusta Savage (1892-1962)

Harlem Renaissance sculptor who shaped raw clay into powerful portraits of Black dignity. Her 16-foot sculpture *The Harp* (inspired by "Lift Every Voice and Sing") was the most popular attraction at the 1939 World's Fair. She founded the Savage Studio of Arts and Crafts, teaching a generation of Black artists. She took raw material and shaped it into art on the front end — for the public to see.
