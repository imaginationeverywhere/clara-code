# Jan — Jan Ernst Matzeliger (1852-1889)

Invented the shoe lasting machine, which automated the process of attaching the upper part of a shoe to its sole. Before his invention, hand-lasters could produce 50 pairs per day; his machine produced 700. He revolutionized manufacturing efficiency and reduced shoe prices by 50%, making footwear affordable for working people. He died at 35 from tuberculosis, largely unrecognized in his lifetime.
