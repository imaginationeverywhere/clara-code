# Vibe Professional Economics In Clara AI for Business

**How VPs monetize through Clara AI for Business.** Separate from the reseller channel — VPs are CONTENT PRODUCERS (build agents); resellers are DISTRIBUTION PARTNERS (sell subscriptions). Orthogonal channels.

---

## The Two Channels That Feed Clara AI for Business

```
              ┌─────────────────────────────┐
              │   CLARA AI for BUSINESS     │
              │   (end customer buys a      │
              │    Team subscription)       │
              └─────────────────────────────┘
                          ▲          ▲
                          │          │
          ┌───────────────┘          └───────────────┐
          │                                           │
   VPs (BUILDERS)                            RESELLERS (DISTRIBUTORS)
   Sub: Clara Code                            Sub: none (sign reseller agreement)
   Build: agents + bundles                    Sell: Clara AI subscriptions
   Earn: 85% of license fees +                Earn: wholesale-to-retail spread
         50% of transaction residuals         (see clara-ai-reseller-rates.md)
```

Both channels can operate independently. A single person CAN be both a VP and a Reseller — but the revenue flows are separate.

## VP Revenue Paths Into Clara AI for Business

A Vibe Professional with a Clara Code subscription has **four ways** to earn from the Clara AI for Business market:

### Path 1 — List Individual Agents on the Talent Agency

VP builds a single agent (e.g., "Pookey's Barber Concierge"). Lists it with a creator-set monthly price.

**Example**: Pookey lists Barber Concierge at **$99/mo per licensing business**.

When a barbershop on Clara AI Team Standard licenses it:
- Barbershop pays Clara: $99/mo
- Clara keeps **15%** = $14.85
- Pookey receives **85%** = $84.15
- Consumes 1 of the barbershop's 10 license slots

Pookey earns **passive recurring revenue** for every barbershop that licenses his agent. 100 barbershops = **$8,415/mo** in recurring licensing income.

### Path 2 — List Agent Bundles (Teams) on the Talent Agency

VP builds a BUNDLE — a set of 3-10 agents working together as a mini-team. Lists the bundle with a bundle-level monthly price. Customer pays once, gets the whole team.

**Example**: Pookey builds "Complete Salon Suite" — 5 agents:
1. Booking Concierge
2. Accountant / Bookkeeper
3. Customer Retention Agent
4. Social Media Manager
5. Inventory Tracker

Lists bundle at **$299/mo**.

When a salon on Clara AI Team Plus licenses the bundle:
- Salon pays Clara: $299/mo
- Clara keeps **15%** = $44.85
- Pookey receives **85%** = $254.15
- Consumes **5** of the salon's 25 license slots (one per agent in bundle)

Pookey sells a "turnkey business team" — one checkout, customer gets a fully-operating back office.

**Bundles scale better for VPs**: easier upsell at higher price points, same 85/15 economics.

### Path 3 — Transaction Residuals (The Passive Income Layer)

When the licensing business's customers actually USE the licensed agent to process a transaction (e.g., a customer books a haircut through Pookey's agent), the **7% Heru platform fee** applies to that transaction.

That 7% is **SPLIT 50/50 between Clara and the VP** (creator of the licensed agent).

**Example**: Barbershop B licenses Pookey's Barber Concierge. Their customer Jennifer books a $42 haircut through the agent.

```
Jennifer pays:
  Haircut listed price              $42.00
  Platform fee (7%)                  $2.94   ← SPLIT between Clara + Pookey
  Processing fee (2.9% + $0.30)      $1.52
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Total                             $46.46

Barbershop B receives: $42.00 (100% of listed price)
Clara receives:         $1.47 (50% of the 7% platform fee)
Pookey receives:        $1.47 (50% of the 7% platform fee) — PASSIVE ROYALTY
Stripe receives:        $1.52
```

Pookey earns **every time Barbershop B does a booking**, forever. Barbershop B doesn't see this breakdown — they only see "processing fee" on their customer's receipt.

**Economic impact**: If Barbershop B does 500 bookings/mo averaging $35, Pookey earns roughly:
- 500 × $35 × 7% × 50% = **$61.25/mo** in transaction royalties — on TOP of the $84.15 license fee
- Total per licensing shop: ~$145/mo recurring
- Pookey scales: 100 shops × $145 = **$14,500/mo recurring** from one agent

### Path 4 — White-Glove Custom Builds (Off-Platform)

VP is hired directly by a specific business to build custom agents. Delivered privately, outside the Talent Agency listing flow.

**Two sub-options:**

**4a. Delivered through Clara AI (hosted on Clara infra)**:
- Business pays VP directly (contract, one-time, etc.)
- Agents run on Clara's infrastructure (via Business Pro/Elite tier)
- Clara keeps 7% transactional fee (standard Heru flow)
- VP keeps whatever they negotiated directly with the business

**4b. Delivered off-platform (VP ejects or delivers outside Clara)**:
- VP ejects agents, ships them to customer's own infra
- Clara gets 0% ongoing
- VP's contract with customer is fully their own
- Only constraint: VP can't have the same agent running on Clara AND a competing platform simultaneously (anti-double-hosting)

## How Path 1/2/3 Stacks — A Full VP Revenue Model

A VP with 3 listed agents on Talent Agency, 100 businesses licensing, averaging 300 bookings/mo each:

```
Per VP Agent (100 licensees):
  License fees:              100 × $99 × 85%       = $8,415/mo
  Transaction royalties:     100 × 300 × $35 × 7% × 50% = $3,675/mo
  Per-agent monthly revenue: $12,090/mo

VP Total (3 agents):        $36,270/mo

VP's Clara Code subscription cost:  $299/mo (Business tier for building)
VP's net: $36,000+/mo from a 3-agent portfolio, sustained monthly
```

**This is the Vibe Professional gig income story made real.** Build 3 agents, list them, business owners license them, you earn recurring forever.

## Reseller Channel — Stays Separate

Resellers do NOT build agents. They SELL subscriptions. See `pricing/clara-ai-reseller-rates.md` for full reseller economics.

A reseller might sell Clara AI Team Standard to 100 barbershops. Each barbershop:
- Pays the reseller (at or below our retail $29/seat)
- Reseller pays Clara wholesale ($20/seat at Team Standard White Label)
- Reseller keeps the $9/seat spread

**Reseller and VP don't conflict**:
- Reseller sells the Team Standard subscription ($29/seat)
- Then that same barbershop USES 10 license slots from Talent Agency, licensing VP-built agents
- Everyone wins: VP earns 85% of license fees, Clara gets 15%, Reseller earned their subscription spread at signup and ongoing
- 7% transactional fees split between Clara (50%) + VP (50%) when the licensed agent processes a transaction

## Combined Example — One Barbershop, All Channels Active

Barbershop with 6 employees:
1. **Reseller-acquired**: a local agency signed them up on Clara AI Team Standard (6 × $29 = $174/mo)
2. **VP-licensed**: they licensed Pookey's Barber Concierge ($99/mo to Pookey via Clara)
3. **Transactional**: 500 customer bookings/mo × $35 × 7% = $122.50 platform fee (Clara $61.25, Pookey $61.25)

```
Barbershop monthly out-of-pocket: $174 + $99 = $273
Plus 7% + Stripe fees on $17,500 in bookings (passed to customers, not shop cost)

Clara monthly revenue from this shop:
  Subscription wholesale retained: $120 (from reseller's wholesale payment — reseller took $54 spread)
  License cut (15% of $99): $14.85
  Transaction platform cut (50% of $122.50): $61.25
  Total Clara: $196.10/mo

Reseller earned: $54 at signup + ongoing $54/mo spread
Pookey (VP) earned: $84.15 license + $61.25 transactional = $145.40/mo recurring
Shop stays ahead: great AI team, passes cost to customers, keeps margin
```

Everyone wins. Classic marketplace economics.

## Key Rules

1. **VPs and Resellers are separate channels** — same person can do both but economics don't cross-contaminate.
2. **85/15 is the license split**: VP keeps 85%, Clara keeps 15% of marketplace licensing revenue.
3. **50/50 is the transactional platform fee split**: of the 7% transaction fee, Clara and VP each get 50%.
4. **License slots are counted by agent count**: a 5-agent bundle consumes 5 slots of a business's license pool.
5. **Off-platform ejection voids Clara's cut**: if VP ejects the agent and the business runs it on their own infra, Clara gets 0%.
6. **Anti-double-hosting rule**: VP can't have the same agent actively serving on Clara AND a competing platform simultaneously.
7. **Talent Agency is the listing surface**: VP lists individual agents OR bundles; businesses browse and license through their Clara AI for Business app.
